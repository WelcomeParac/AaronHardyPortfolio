Experience the transformation of Sunbelt Bakery's granola bar packaging as it evolves to meet the demands of a competitive market
Project Details / Background
Problem:

Original Packaging Felt Uninspiring and Outdated

Sunbelt Bakery's original granola bar packaging design was perceived as uninspiring and old-fashioned, which hindered its appeal to modern consumers. The packaging lacked modern branding elements, including storytelling, mission-driven messaging, and bold visual appeal. It may have been too simple and conservative, failing to stand out on the competitive store shelves.

Role: Sole designer and researcher

Duration: 3 weeks

Tools:

1. Market Research: Conducted in-depth research on Sunbelt Bakery's competitors to gain insights into successful packaging strategies.

2. Design Software: Utilized Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator and Adobe Dimensions to create a new packaging concept that includes brighter colors and a minimalist look.

3. Color Psychology Knowledge: Applied principles of color psychology to select suitable colors that elicit desired emotional responses from consumers.

4. Minimalist Design Principles: Employed principles of minimalism to create a clutter-free and appealing packaging design.

Solution:

Redesigned Packaging with Brighter Colors and Minimalist Look

1. Developed a new packaging design featuring brighter and more vibrant colors to enhance visibility and attract consumer attention.

2. Adopted a minimalist design approach, which aligns with modern design trends, giving the packaging a contemporary and sophisticated look.

3. Ensured effective communication by prominently displaying key information, such as product benefits and ingredients.

4. Focused on reinforcing Sunbelt Bakery's brand identity while keeping the design fresh and appealing.

5. Planned for consistency and versatility to adapt the new design across the product range.

Research:

Competitive Analysis and Brand Insights

1.Conducted a thorough analysis of Sunbelt Bakery's competitors, including Nature Valley, Kind Bars, Clif Bar, RXBAR, and Kashi, to understand their successful packaging strategies.

2. Researched the market trends and preferences of consumers seeking granola bars, health-conscious snacks, and wholesome food options.

3. Explored how competitors effectively conveyed their brand stories, values, and health-related benefits through packaging design.

4. Examined the principles of color psychology and minimalist design to inform the choices made in the redesigned packaging.

Challenges:

1. Market Competition: Competing in a saturated granola bar market with strong competitors posed a significant challenge. Creating a distinct and compelling packaging design that could stand out amid fierce competition required innovative thinking.

2. Implementation and Production: Transitioning to the new packaging design across the entire product range and managing the production process was a logistical challenge. Ensuring consistency in print quality and design across various packaging types and sizes required careful coordination.

3. Balancing Tradition with Modernity: One of the primary challenges was finding the right balance between retaining Sunbelt Bakery's traditional image and infusing a modern and appealing aesthetic. Striking this balance required careful consideration of the brand's heritage while addressing the need for a contemporary look.

Learnings:

1. Adaptability: The packaging redesign project taught us the value of adaptability in a dynamic market. Being open to adjustments based on feedback and market changes is crucial for long-term success.

2. Consistency in Branding: Maintaining consistency in branding elements such as brand colors while adopting a fresh design, is key to reinforcing brand identity and recognition.

3. Market Research: In-depth market research and competitor analysis are essential for understanding industry trends and staying ahead of the Competition.
